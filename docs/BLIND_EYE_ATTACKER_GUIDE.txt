Operative,

Welcome to the other side of the firewall.

You are not a defender. You are not bound by their rules,
their protocols, or their clearance hierarchies. You exist
in the spaces between their monitors, in the gaps between
their log entries, in the seconds between their heartbeats.

This manual will teach you how to exploit every weakness in
a Javanet network. How to crack their puzzles. How to slip
through their defenses. How to make their entire facility
yours without them ever knowing you were there.

Read carefully. There are no second chances in the field.

------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 1: YOUR OFFENSIVE TOOLKIT
------------------------------------------------------------------

RECONNAISSANCE:
  - Scanner: Broadcast probe to find all Javanet computers
    in modem range. Returns their faction name and ID.
  - Interceptor: Raw modem sniffer. Captures ALL traffic
    on any channel you choose. Including their signed
    internal messages.

BREACH:
  - Cracker: The puzzle engine. You vs their security tier.
    Tier 1-2: Easy, fast. Tier 3-4: Hard, long.
    Tier 5: Near impossible. Choose your targets.
  - HMAC Cracker: If you capture a signed message with
    the Interceptor, this tries to brute-force their
    shared secret. Low chance, but game-changing if it works.

EXPLOITATION:
  - Card Spoofer: Clone, forge, or create burn cards.
  - Payload Deployer: Push lockout, worm, backdoor, or
    agent scripts to compromised machines.
  - Worm Commander: Control your deployed worms. Authorize
    spread to new targets with mini-puzzles.
  - Agent Control: Manage stealth event hooks on targets.

DISRUPTION:
  - Signal Jammer: Flood modem traffic. Causes timeouts
    and lag across their entire network.
  - Keylogger: Disguise as a door terminal. Capture every
    card swipe. Get disk IDs for cloning.

SECTION 2: THE ATTACK PLAYBOOK
------------------------------------------------------------------

PHASE 1 — RECONNAISSANCE (5-10 min)
  1. Set up a hidden terminal away from the facility
  2. Run Scanner to find all targets
  3. Run Interceptor to capture traffic patterns
  4. Note: which computers are doors? Which is mainframe?
  5. Map the network before you touch anything

PHASE 2 — INITIAL ACCESS (varies)
  Option A: PUZZLE CRACK
    - Target a low-tier door (T1-T2)
    - Solve the puzzle, gain a session token
    - Session lasts 5 minutes — work fast

  Option B: CARD FORGERY
    - If you captured card auth traffic with Interceptor,
      use Card Spoofer to clone a card
    - Walk up to a door and swipe like you belong there
    - Clone = undetectable. Forge = flagged by IDS.

  Option C: KEYLOGGER TRAP
    - Place a computer near a door they use
    - Install keylogger module, set fake zone name
    - Wait for someone to swipe their card
    - Clone the captured disk ID

  Option D: BURN CARD
    - Solve a T2 puzzle to create a one-time-use card
    - Single swipe, then the card self-destructs
    - Completely untraceable

PHASE 3 — ESTABLISH PERSISTENCE
  1. Deploy AGENT first (stealth priority)
     - Hooks os.pullEventRaw
     - Reports card swipes back to you
     - Nearly invisible to antivirus scans
  2. Deploy BACKDOOR second
     - Listens for remote commands
     - Can open doors, reboot, exfiltrate data
  3. Deploy WORM only when ready to go loud
     - Probes adjacent computers automatically
     - YOU control spread via Worm Commander
     - Each spread requires a mini-puzzle (T1-T2)

PHASE 4 — ESCALATION
  - Use worm network to reach deeper targets
  - Exfiltrate config files, personnel lists, logs
  - Clone admin-level cards from captured data
  - Crack the mainframe (usually T4-T5)

PHASE 5 — TAKEOVER
  - Deploy LOCKOUT on all infected machines at once
  - Your faction logo, your colors, your message
  - Every screen in the facility shows YOUR face
  - The defenders are blind

SECTION 3: SECRETS THEY DON'T KNOW
------------------------------------------------------------------

SECRET 1: STEALTH PROTOCOL
  The Interceptor and Scanner can use raw modem.transmit()
  instead of rednet. Standard IDS cannot see raw modem
  traffic. Only the Deep Scanner module catches it — and
  most defenders don't install it. Use stealth mode for
  all reconnaissance to stay invisible.

SECRET 2: THE COOLDOWN EXPLOIT
  Cooldowns are per-attacker, per-resource. If you have
  TWO computers, you can attack from both simultaneously.
  Computer A gets cooldown? Switch to Computer B. Double
  your attack speed, and the logs show different source IDs.

SECRET 3: BURN CARDS ARE UNTRACEABLE
  Forged cards show up as unknown IDs in the IDS. But burn
  cards self-destruct after one use AND leave no trace in
  the personnel database. The only way to catch a burn card
  is if the IDS happens to catch the auth in real-time.

SECRET 4: AGENT VS WORM — CHOOSE WISELY
  - Agent: Silent. Nearly undetectable. Good for long-term
    intelligence gathering. Survives antivirus scans better.
  - Worm: Loud. Spreads fast. Good for takeovers. But the
    dofile() hook in startup.lua is easy for defenders to
    find with Integrity Check.
  Use agents for recon. Use worms for the final push.

SECRET 5: THE HONEYPOT TELL
  Defenders set up honeypots — fake terminals with tempting
  names like "ADMIN_CONSOLE" or "VAULT_CONTROL." How to spot
  them: probe the target, then wait 30+ seconds. Honeypots
  respond SLOWLY on purpose to waste your time. Real terminals
  respond in under 5 seconds. If it's slow, it's a trap.

SECRET 6: MAINFRAME CRACK SEQUENCE
  The mainframe is usually T4-T5 security. But individual
  DOORS on the mainframe's network might only be T1-T2.
  Crack a door first, deploy a worm, spread to the mainframe
  from inside the network. Easier than a direct crack.

SECRET 7: THE REPLAY WINDOW
  Signed messages have a 10-second replay window. If you
  capture a signed "open door" message with the Interceptor,
  you have 10 seconds to replay it with the Replayer module.
  This bypasses the puzzle entirely. Timing is everything.

SECRET 8: LOCKOUT PERSISTENCE
  The lockout payload traps os.pullEventRaw. Even Ctrl+T
  only gets a taunt message and a 2-second delay before
  breaking out. During those 2 seconds, a defender can't
  do anything. If you time a lockout with a worm spread,
  defenders can't respond fast enough.

SECRET 9: DUAL APPROACH
  Run your attack terminal with BOTH offense AND defense
  modules. Put a Firewall on your own hacking station.
  If they try to trace or counter-hack you, YOUR firewall
  blocks their attempts. You're not just attacking — you're
  defending your attack position.

SECRET 10: THE JAMMER DISTRACTION
  Signal Jammer floods one modem channel. Defenders will
  rush to investigate the "network issues" while you
  quietly crack a door on a different channel. Use the
  jammer as a feint, not a primary weapon.

SECTION 4: PUZZLE TIPS
------------------------------------------------------------------

TIER 1 — PATTERN MATCH (15-30 sec, 5 attempts)
  - Just look for the repeating pattern
  - You have 5 tries — experiment freely
  - Fastest tier. Don't overthink it.

TIER 2 — PORT SEQUENCE (30-60 sec, 3 attempts)
  - Find the mathematical rule: add, multiply, fibonacci
  - Common rules: +3, +5, x2, x2+1, fib
  - Check: does each number relate to the one before it?
  - Only 3 attempts. Think before you type.

TIER 3 — SIGNAL ROUTER (1-3 min, 3 attempts)
  - 3 stages of increasing complexity
  - Toggle gates ON/OFF to route signal from IN to OUT
  - Start from the OUTPUT and work backwards
  - Each stage is independent — solving one doesn't help next

TIER 4 — CIPHER CRACK (3-5 min, 2 attempts)
  - Caesar cipher with known shift value
  - Tab opens frequency analysis tool
  - Shift each letter BACKWARDS by the shift amount
  - Example: shift +3, encrypted "G" = plain "D"
  - Only 2 attempts. Get it right.

TIER 5 — SYSTEM SIEGE (5-10 min, 1 attempt per phase)
  - Chain of T2 -> T3 -> T4. Fail ANY and start over.
  - Full regeneration on failure — can't memorize answers
  - This is the boss fight. Bring coffee.
  - Tip: the time limit is shared across ALL phases.
    Rush the T2, take your time on T4.
